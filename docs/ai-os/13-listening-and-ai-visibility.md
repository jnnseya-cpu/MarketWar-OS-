## The strategic thesis (adopted)

BLG and Brandwatch are mirror images — one **executes** (content + a 4,000+
site backlink network + autopilot AI-visibility tracking) but never listens;
the other **listens** (1.4T conversations, 500M+/day) but never executes. Their
2026 convergence (Brandwatch adding GEO measurement of ChatGPT/Perplexity
recommendation) proves the categories are merging. **MarketWar OS plants its
flag at the intersection: listen → execute → get recommended by AI engines, in
one autonomous OS.** This extends the platform from a customer-acquisition OS
into also owning the social-listening + AI-visibility (GEO) authority space.

This composes with what is already shipped/blueprinted, not replacing it:
- **SiteRaid AI** (doc 10 Part E) already has a GEO / Generative-Search
  Visibility engine and the "plain input, expert output" pattern.
- **Competitor Spy** (`competitor-spy`) and the **Amplification engine**
  (doc 12) already touch competitor/creative intelligence and distribution.
- The **Africa corridor** (doc 08 §C.3) and **BitriPay** (doc 05) are the
  substrate for the Africa Theatre Pack.

## Two asymmetric advantages (adopted as strategy)

1. **Portfolio-blog cold-start solve** — the owner's portfolio blogs seed the
   authority/backlink network on day one; BLG needed years to build its 4,000
   sites. Record as a genuine moat.
2. **Africa Theatre Pack** — French/Lingala/Swahili listening + DRC publisher
   inventory + BitriPay attribution — a front where both incumbents are absent
   (BLG: 50+ languages, zero African; Brandwatch: DRC coverage ~nil). This is
   the same defensible corridor the v3 spec named Phase-4.

## The wedge (adopted)

**MW-07 Query Compiler** — natural-language → Boolean listening query, so
non-analysts get analyst-grade listening. Removes Brandwatch Iris's
"analysts-who-speak-Boolean" dependency (its stated weakness). Sibling to the
SiteRaid approach. *(Full MW-07 spec pending dossier body.)*
