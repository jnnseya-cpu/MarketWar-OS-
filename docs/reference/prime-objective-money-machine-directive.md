## 1. The Prime Objective

MarketWar OS exists for one reason only: **to make its user money.** Every agent, screen, notification and line of code answers one question before it exists: *"Does this put money in the user's account, or move money measurably closer to it?"* If no, it does not ship. MarketWar is not a marketing tool the user operates — it is a revenue machine that operates for the user.

Formally: MarketWar OS shall increase the user's customer intake, conversion rate, average transaction value, purchase frequency, and net revenue — visibly, measurably, attributably — from Day 1, and compound that advantage until the user is the dominant operator in their niche and geography.

## 2. The Money Machine Doctrine — Seven Laws

1. **Day-1 Money Law.** A revenue-relevant result within 24h of onboarding: a lead captured, a dormant customer reactivated, an abandoned sale recovered, or a competitor weakness exposed with a ready counter-offer. No setup weeks. The machine earns before it explains.
2. **Attribution Law.** Every pound/dollar/franc generated is tracked, stamped and shown in a live **Money Ledger**: *"MarketWar generated £4,230 for you this month. Your subscription cost £199. ROI: 21.3x."* This single screen is the retention engine — nobody cancels a machine that prints its own receipts.
3. **Full-Funnel Ownership Law.** Owns the entire money pipeline: attention → lead → conversation → sale → repeat sale → referral. A lead that doesn't become revenue is a system failure; agents pursue it until it converts, is monetised another way, or is formally disqualified with a reason.
4. **Compounding Law.** Every campaign makes the next smarter; every customer won teaches who the next hundred are; every objection handled becomes a permanent counter-script. Dependency comes from escalating, provable results — not lock-in tricks.
5. **Niche Domination Law.** Built to make competition irrelevant. Maps every rival in the user's field and postcode/commune, monitors pricing/offers/reviews/ads/weaknesses near-real-time, and auto-drafts strike responses: counter-offers, comparison pages, review-response campaigns, conquest ads at rivals' unhappy customers.
6. **Speed-of-Money Law.** Cash velocity beats volume. Prioritise by speed-to-revenue: reactivate past customers before cold acquisition; upsells before new funnels; abandoned-cart recovery before brand campaigns. Fastest money first, always.
7. **Expansion Law.** Once ROI is proven on one stream, proactively propose the next: new offer, segment, channel, or a data-supported price increase. The rational monthly decision should be to spend more on MarketWar — because every extra ACU returns a multiple.

## 3. The Value Engine — the permanent five-stage revenue loop

1. **HUNT (Customer Intake):** identify exactly who buys, where, what they respond to; flood intake — local SEO, social engines, conquest campaigns, referral loops, partnership prospecting.
2. **CAPTURE (Lead → Conversation):** capture every visitor/caller/DM/walk-in; AI responders answer in seconds, 24/7, in the customer's language (English/French/Lingala/Swahili for the DRC corridor); qualify + book.
3. **CLOSE (Conversation → Cash):** objection-handling, offer sequencing, urgency mechanics, follow-up cadences that never tire — 7, 12, 20 touches if needed. The machine never stops chasing.
4. **MULTIPLY (Cash → More Cash Per Customer):** auto upsell/cross-sell/bundle/repeat engines; price intelligence for the max the market happily pays; AOV + frequency as dials turned up monthly.
5. **FORTIFY (Customers → Assets):** review generation for local ranking dominance, referral weaponisation, win-back for lapsed customers, loyalty mechanics — each satisfied customer recruits the next at near-zero cost.

The loop reports its kills to the Money Ledger daily.

## 4. Day 1 → Day 90: the Dependency Curve

- **Day 1 — First Blood.** <20-min ingestion; before day-end: dormant-reactivation blast fired, abandoned pipeline recovered, competitor map generated, first counter-offer ready. Money movement on Day 1.
- **Week 1 — Proof.** Money Ledger shows first attributed revenue; first War Report (money generated, leads captured, competitor moves, next strikes).
- **Month 1 — Habit.** User checks MarketWar like their bank account; ROI multiple permanent; first expansion proposal.
- **Month 3 — Dependency.** Machine holds the entire commercial memory; turning it off amputates the revenue nervous system. Retention is arithmetic.

## 5. The Competition-Kill System

The niche is a battlefield with finite demand; the OS captures a disproportionate share. **War Room** keeps a live dossier on every competitor (offers, prices, ad angles, review sentiment, response times, weaknesses) and generates strike packages: conquest campaigns at rivals' dissatisfied customers, comparison assets, counter-offers within hours of a competitor promo, review-dominance campaigns. End-state: in the user's niche + territory, MarketWar users become unbearable to compete against — faster, louder, better-rated, better-priced, everywhere the customer looks.

## 7. Monetisation — the machine that sells itself

- **BitriPay rail (portfolio standard):** all customer payments, campaign spend, subscription billing flow through BitriPay — UK/EU cards + DRC mobile money (M-Pesa, Orange, Airtel, Africell) — deployable Birmingham → Kinshasa on day one.
- **ACU billing (3× multiplier rule):** every agent action consumes AI Compute Units. Because LEDGER proves revenue per ACU, buying more ACUs is presented as an investment with displayed historical return: *"Your last 5,000 ACUs generated £Y. Reload?"*
- **Tier logic:** entry tier pays for itself within week 1 (Law 1); growth tiers unlock divisions (War Room, Expansion Command); Dominator unlocks full 26-agent deployment with priority ACU throughput. Every boundary sits where the Ledger makes the upgrade arithmetic undeniable.

## 8. North-Star Metrics (shown permanently)

1. **Attributed Revenue Generated** (headline — the machine's paycheque to the user)
2. **ROI Multiple** (revenue generated ÷ total MarketWar spend)
3. **New Customers Acquired** (intake velocity)
4. **Revenue Per Customer** (multiplication effect)
5. **Territory Share** (visibility + rating dominance vs mapped competitors)

Internal product north star: **% of users with ≥5× ROI displayed by Day 30.**
