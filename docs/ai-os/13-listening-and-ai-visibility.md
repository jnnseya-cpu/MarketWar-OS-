## Positioning

**MarketWar Organic Dominance OS** (internal: Market Intelligence, Search &
Demand Capture Engine — **MISDCE**) is not an SEO writer, social-listening
dashboard, scheduler or analytics tool. It is an autonomous marketing
intelligence **and execution** system: *"listens to the market, predicts
demand, creates the response, distributes it everywhere and converts attention
into measurable revenue."*

**The winning operating loop (the competitive advantage):**
`Listen → Predict → Decide → Create → Publish → Engage → Capture → Convert →
Attribute → Optimise` — versus BLG's `Analyse→Write→Publish→Authority→Track`
(executes, never listens) and Brandwatch's `Collect→Filter→Analyse→Visualise→
Alert→Human decision` (listens, never executes). MarketWar closes both gaps in
one OS. This is the flag doc 13 plants at the merging listen/execute/AI-answer
category, and it directly serves the self-growth doctrine (doc 01 §1.6).

## Adopted engine contracts (Section 1)

- **Command Centre (§4, binding principle):** never a passive dashboard —
  every insight carries a recommended action + a one-click/autonomous
  execution option. **23 primary metrics** (Market Attention → Campaign
  Opportunity Pipeline, incl. AI Answer Visibility Score, Purchase Intent
  Volume, Unanswered Demand, Estimated Revenue Opportunity, Cost per Organic
  Lead, Content ROI). Daily **"What changed?" briefing** (10 elements) and a
  **14-action one-click set**.
- **Autonomous onboarding (§5):** 18 accepted inputs → 17 built artefacts
  (business/brand-voice/visual-DNA profiles, product-service graph, keyword +
  customer-question + AI-prompt + social-query universes, reputation-risk
  dictionary, content pillars, 90-day plan). Website crawler extracts 23
  elements — composes with the shipped SiteRaid crawler contract (doc 10 §E).
- **Market Listening engine (§6):** ~31 source categories **subject to lawful
  platform access, licensing and API limits** — the dossier's own safeguard
  (source-specific permissions, retention rules, fallback collection; never
  promise unrestricted access) is binding and aligns with the platform's
  consent/compliance architecture (doc 08 §B). Query builder: **Simple mode**
  (AI builds the query from 8 inputs) + **Expert mode** (17 Boolean/facet
  controls) + **smart expansion** (13 classes incl. slang, phonetic, local-
  language) — critical for the FR/EN/Lingala/Swahili code-switching African
  markets (ties to the Africa Theatre Pack + doc 08 §C.3).
- **Conversation Intelligence (§7):** every mention enriched with 25
  attributes; 13 emotion classes; 16 intent classes (incl. ready-to-buy,
  switching-competitor, potential-crisis, potential-fraud).
- **Purchase-Intent & Lead-Detection (§8):** detects commercially-valuable
  statements ("can anyone recommend…", "looking for a supplier for…", "…in
  Birmingham/Kinshasa?"). **Lead Opportunity Card** (18 fields incl. estimated
  value, compliance status, follow-up deadline) + 10 execution options
  (draft reply/DM, create CRM lead, trigger WhatsApp, or **monitor-without-
  responding / exclude sensitive** — consent-safe by design).
- **Search Intelligence (§9):** 19 traditional signals + a **Prompt Universe**
  (12 prompt types) with 10-factor prompt-opportunity scoring — the AI-search
  successor to keyword tools.
- **Generative Search Visibility (§10):** monitors 9 AI answer platforms
  (Google AI Overviews, ChatGPT, Perplexity, Copilot, Gemini, Claude, …)
  across 13 visibility measurements; **AI Answer Accuracy Monitor** (8
  detections → 7 correction actions). **Causal-measurement safeguard (binding
  honesty rule):** never claim all AI-referral growth came from our work —
  compare against untreated pages, historical baselines and control groups.
  This is the credibility wedge over raw-traffic-only competitors, and it
  matches the platform's honest-measurement doctrine.
- **Technical SEO/GEO/AEO Auditor (§11):** 5 audit categories (crawlability,
  page structure, performance/CWV, structured data ×15 schema types, AI-
  readiness incl. llms.txt) with a **Fix mode** (severity, business impact,
  auto-fix eligibility, before/after preview, rollback).
- **Competitor War Room (§12):** 20 monitored signals per competitor +
  Weakness Scanner (14 weakness classes) + 10 one-click exploitation actions —
  extends shipped Competitor Spy + SiteRaid Attack Map.
- **Content Opportunity Radar (§13):** merges 12 signal sources into 14
  opportunity categories with a transparent, editable score:
  `Opportunity Score = Demand × Commercial Intent × Relevance × Timing ×
  Authority Probability × Conversion Probability ÷ Competition`.
- **Autonomous Content Factory (§14):** 26 output types (SEO/GEO/AEO articles,
  comparison + alternative pages, case studies, video/podcast scripts, lead
  magnets, white papers…). Content-generation controls **(list continues in
  Section 2 — paste ends mid-item at "Claims")**.
